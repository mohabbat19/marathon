## Workout Types

- **Easy Run**: Conversational pace (60-70% max HR)
- **Moderate Run**: Steady pace (70-75% max HR)
- **Tempo Run**: Comfortably hard (80-85% max HR)
- **Long Run**: Build endurance, practice fueling
- **Hill Repeats**: 8-10 Ã— 90 sec uphill
- **Cross-Training**: Cycling, swimming, yoga
- **Rest Day**: Complete rest or light stretching

## Fueling Guidelines

### Pre-Run (2-3 hours before)
- Bagel with peanut butter
- Oatmeal with banana
- Toast with jam

### During Run (every 45 min for runs >90 min)
- Energy gel or chews (100-150 cal)
- Water or sports drink (4-8 oz)

### Post-Run (within 30 min)
- Protein + Carbs
- 20-30g protein
- Examples: chocolate milk, protein shake, sandwich

## Hydration

- **Daily**: Half your body weight in ounces
- **During runs**: 6-8 oz every 15-20 minutes
- **After runs**: Add 16-24 oz per hour of running

## Injury Prevention

### Daily
- Dynamic warm-up (5-10 min before runs)
- Static stretching (after runs, hold 30-45 sec)

### Weekly
- Strength training 2-3Ã— (core, glutes, legs)
- Foam rolling 10-15 min

### Signs to Stop
- Sharp, sudden pain
- Pain that worsens while running
- Swelling or discoloration
- Pain that changes your gait

## Recovery Week Protocol
Every 3-4 weeks, reduce mileage 20-30%:
- Shorter long run
- Keep easy runs easy
- Extra rest day if needed
- Focus on sleep and nutrition

## Pacing Strategy

### Miles 1-6: Conservative Start
- 15-20 sec SLOWER than goal pace
- Settle in, don't get excited
- Bank energy for later

### Miles 7-20: Settle In
- Move to goal pace or 5-10 sec slower
- Should feel comfortable
- Maintain fueling schedule

### Miles 20-23: The Wall
- Focus on maintaining pace
- Break into 1-mile chunks
- Use positive self-talk
- This is the real race

### Miles 23-26.2: The Finish
- Gradually increase if you can
- Give everything in final 0.2
- Enjoy crossing that line!

## Mental Mantras
- "I am strong, I am trained, I am ready"
- "One mile at a time"
- "My training prepared me for this"
- "Temporary discomfort, not permanent pain"
- "I've come too far to stop now"

## Key Numbers to Remember

- **10% Rule**: Max weekly mileage increase
- **3:4 Ratio**: 3 build weeks : 1 recovery week
- **90+ minutes**: Start fueling during runs
- **48 hours**: Recovery time for hard workouts
- **7-9 hours**: Ideal sleep for training
- **300-500 miles**: Replace running shoes
- **26.2 miles**: Your goal!
